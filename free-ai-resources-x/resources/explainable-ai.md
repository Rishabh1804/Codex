# Explainable AI (XAI)

> Model interpretability, transparency, and techniques to understand why AI systems make their decisions.

### Courses

- [Coursera: Interpretable Machine Learning](https://www.coursera.org/learn/interpretable-machine-learning) - Free to audit course covering major XAI techniques and frameworks. `Intermediate`
- [Google: Explainable AI on Google Cloud](https://cloud.google.com/explainable-ai) - Free tools, documentation, and guides for interpretable AI. `Intermediate`
- [Harvard: Interpretability in Machine Learning](https://interpretable-ml-class.github.io/) - Full course materials on ML interpretability methods. `Advanced`

### Books & Reading

- [Interpretable Machine Learning (Christoph Molnar)](https://christophm.github.io/interpretable-ml-book/) - The go-to free book on model interpretability — comprehensive and regularly updated. `Intermediate`
- [Fairness and Machine Learning (Barocas, Hardt, Narayanan)](https://fairmlbook.org/) - Free textbook on fairness, bias, and accountability in ML. `Intermediate`
- [Explanatory Model Analysis (Biecek & Burzykowski)](https://ema.drwhy.ai/) - Free book exploring model explanations with R examples. `Intermediate`

### Tools & Libraries

- [SHAP (SHapley Additive exPlanations)](https://shap.readthedocs.io/en/latest/) - Leading explainability library with tutorials and visualizations. `Intermediate`
- [LIME (Local Interpretable Model-agnostic Explanations)](https://lime-ml.readthedocs.io/en/latest/) - Tools and tutorials for explaining individual predictions. `Intermediate`
- [Captum (PyTorch)](https://captum.ai/) - Model interpretability library for PyTorch with comprehensive tutorials. `Intermediate`
- [InterpretML (Microsoft)](https://interpret.ml/) - Open-source toolkit for training interpretable models and explaining blackbox systems. `Intermediate`
- [Alibi Explain](https://docs.seldon.io/projects/alibi/en/stable/) - Open-source library of ML model inspection and interpretation algorithms. `Advanced`
- [What-If Tool (Google)](https://pair-code.github.io/what-if-tool/) - Interactive visual tool for exploring ML model behavior without code. `Beginner`
